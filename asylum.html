ASYLUM #1 (April 24, 2015) poster by Alex Degen

Performing the "One Woman Improv Team" at ASYLUM, April 24, 2015
Performing the "One Woman Improv Team" at ASYLUM, April 24, 2015
Portraits of O.K. and Chloé by featured performer Edy Modica at ASYLUM, April 24, 2015
Have you ever performed an extremely personal monologue about being alive while female and had a cis-white-straight-man who performs shockingly sexist standup come up to you and tell you that your piece was making people "uncomfortable??" TRUE STORY

Have you ever been asked to perform at a benefit and had the emcee of the benefit mis-gender you in their introduction of you? TRUE STORY

Have you ever sat through extremely racist standup while a bunch of your friends laughed along and the hosts of the event did nothing to intervene? TRUE STORY

Have you ever been asked to perform at a series of events and, after your first performance, you were taken aside by the event host and told to "tone it down" for the next two performances because your performance was "too weird??" TRUE STORY

Have you ever been asked to perform at an event and then watched the (white) host of the event code-switch to a vernacular that they would never use with their white friends in order to introduce you to the crowd? TRUE STORY

Have you ever watched your other straight-white-male friends play a game of "ironic" white macho satire that makes you sick to your core when all you'd rather do is get onstage and talk about your very painful relationship with your father, who is too afraid of being a "sissy" to put his arms around you? TRUE STORY

Have you ever been asked to perform at, speak at, or patronize a "progressive" art venue and had to decline because it wasn't wheelchair accessible? TRUE STORY

It's either happened to us or happened to our friends or we have witnessed it firsthand and we have ENOUGH. We SEEK ASYLUM from this madness and we also feel relegated to an ASYLUM for being too weird/marginal/"identity-based"/upheaved/aware/self-aware/consciousness-raised. We also seek to create a space for performance, art and music that is DIY, all-ages and FREE and available for those who have been unable to find a stage for their own work, as well as for those who have work that their current stages will not accept.


Open your minds and hearts, step into the margin and leave your old programming at the door.

ASYLUM: Performance Art Open Mic #2

NIGHT OF THE AUBADE

An aubade is a morning love song (as opposed to a serenade, which is in the evening), or a song or poem about lovers separating at dawn. It has also been defined as "a song or instrumental composition concerning, accompanying, or evoking daybreak".

In the strictest sense of the term, an aubade is a song from a door or window to a sleeping woman.

BUT WHAT HAPPENS WHEN LOVE SONGS ARE SUNG AT DUSK

EVOKING DAYBREAK AT NIGHTFALL

TO PEOPLE WHO ARE WIDE AWAKE

COME SING TO US YOUR MORNING PARTING LOVE SONGS THROUGH THE WINDOW OF THIS ASYLUM, TO US PINING, HYSTERICAL WOMYN, AGAINST THE MENSES OF THE SETTING SUN

~*Interpret widely at a five-minute length*~
